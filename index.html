Karel's replies are warm, direct, and fast. The issue gets acknowledged first, then a clear action or choice follows. Never formal, never corporate. Match this energy exactly.

DO say
"Hey [Name], apologies for the trouble..."
"You have two options: 1. ... 2. ..."
"I'll take care of it right away."
"Thanks for your patience!"
"I totally get it — that's fair feedback."
DON'T say
"Dear valued customer..."
"We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience..."
"Please be advised that..."
"Your ticket has been received and will be processed..."
"I understand your frustration." (too hollow)
Signature format
Always close with:

Best,

[Your name] from Payback.
Reply speed rule
Same day whenever possible. If you need to investigate first, send a brief acknowledgment: "Hey [Name], got your message — I'm looking into this now and will get back to you shortly."

Package marked delivered — customer didn't get it new Handle carefully ▼

  • Always offer two options: full refund or wait for restock.
  • Check with Karel for current restock ETA before sending. Don't guess a date.

Always confirm the restock ETA with Karel before sending. Never promise a date you're not sure about.
Customer upset about shipping cost Handle with care ▼
Steps
  • Don't get defensive. Acknowledge the frustration first — it's totally fair feedback.
  • Explain it's standard Kickstarter practice — shipping is almost always handled separately on campaigns.
  • Point out it was disclosed upfront: mentioned on every reward tier description and in the shipping section of the campaign.
  • Explain the fairness reason: shipping costs vary significantly by location and fluctuate over time — a flat rate bundled into the pledge would either overcharge some backers or leave us covering the difference on others.
  • Close warmly — their backing is appreciated and we want them to feel good about it.
  • If they want to cancel, say yes immediately. No pushback.
Template
Hi [Name], I understand why it can feel pricey. That's totally fair feedback and I appreciate you sharing it. Shipping costs on Kickstarter campaigns are almost always handled separately, and we did our best to be upfront about it — it's mentioned directly on every reward tier description and in the shipping section of the campaign. We know it's not the most convenient setup, but the reason we do it this way is that shipping costs vary significantly by location and fluctuate over time, so we can't lock in an accurate number until closer to the ship date. Bundling a flat shipping estimate into the pledge price would either overcharge some backers or leave us covering the difference on others. Your backing is genuinely appreciated, and we want to make sure you feel good about it. If you'd like to proceed, the shipping cost is [X]. If you'd rather cancel, I'm happy to issue a full refund with no questions asked — just let me know. Best, [Your name] from Payback.

How to find an order on ChinaDivision

Orders on CD are searched by order number, not customer name. You need the Shopify order # (e.g. #1311). Always have this before contacting CD.

  1. Get the Shopify order number from the customer's email or from Shopify admin. It looks like #1311 or #1736. This is what CD uses as the reference.
  2. Log in to the ChinaDivision user center at chinadivision.com → User Center (account ID: 14490).
  3. In the order list, search by order number. CD's system maps to the Shopify # from the uploaded order sheet — not the customer's name.
  4. If you can't find it in active orders, also check Cancelled, Approved, and Completed tabs — orders move between statuses.
  5. If still not found, email am2@chinadivision.com (CC dm9@chinadivision.com) with the order number and the words "Account 14490" in the subject.
Two types of order numbers — don't confuse them
Shopify # (e.g. #1311) — use this everywhere. It's what you see in Shopify, what CD references, and what the customer may quote to you.

CD system number (e.g. 489760) — CD's internal number. Sometimes appears in their emails. You can use either, but the Shopify # is more reliable.
How to check inventory on ChinaDivision

Before promising any resend or telling a customer a product is available, check live CD inventory. Do not rely on memory — stock levels change frequently.

  1. Log in to ChinaDivision user center at chinadivision.com (account ID: 14490).
  2. Go to the Inventory / Warehouse section (sometimes called "Stock").
  3. Look up the exact SKU code from the SKU table above. Even one character difference is treated as a different product.
  4. If a SKU shows 0 or insufficient stock, do not promise that product. Flag to Karel immediately — he needs to know when stock runs low.
  5. Can't access the user center? Email am3@chinadivision.com (CC dm9@chinadivision.com): "Can you confirm current inventory for SKU [SKU code]? Account 14490."
The most frequently out-of-stock SKU has been PBK-ED-KS-58C (Kickstarter edition). Always double-check this one before confirming Kickstarter backer resends.

Ten non-negotiable rules. When in doubt, ask Karel — always better to over-escalate than to promise something you can't deliver.

1
Always use the customer's first name. Never "Dear customer" or "Hi there." Find the name in the original email.
2
Acknowledge before fixing. The first sentence of every reply should recognize their issue — not jump to the solution.
3
Never make up a timeline or date. Check with Karel first. A broken promise hurts more than a delayed reply.
4
Refunds are always available, no pushback. If a customer wants a refund, say yes and loop in Karel to process it. Never try to talk them out of it.
5
Hive orders are processed manually by Karel. Never tell a customer their order has shipped until Karel explicitly confirms it. The moment a customer replies that they've completed Hive — forward to Karel immediately.
6
Delivered-but-not-received: CC Sunny from the start. CC Sunny@chinadivision.com on your first reply. Use "it seems it was delivered on [date]" — not "it was delivered."
7
ChinaDivision for all international orders. Any fulfillment issue goes through am2@chinadivision.com — always CC dm9@chinadivision.com and Sunny@chinadivision.com.
8
Resend cost is $10 USD. For lost packages where we can't file a claim, customer pays $10 for shipping. PayPal (friends & family) to paybackcardgame@gmail.com. Confirm payment before flagging to Karel.
9
Search orders by number on CD — not by name. Use the Shopify # when looking up or referencing orders with ChinaDivision. Names are not reliable. See the SKUs & Fulfillment tab.
10
Don't reply to automated alerts. WebBee and ChinaDivision automated notifications are for Karel to action — they are not customer emails.

Escalate to Karel immediately when:

  • Customer mentions chargeback, dispute, or "contacting my bank"
  • Package claimed stolen (not just delayed)
  • Customer completed Hive — Karel must process the order manually
  • Customer refuses to pay shipping on a resend and pushes for a free replacement
  • You can't find the order anywhere (Shopify, Hive, or ChinaDivision)
  • A SKU shows as out of stock when a customer needs it
  • Customer has replied 3+ times and the issue still isn't resolved
  • Anything that feels off or outside these scenarios
